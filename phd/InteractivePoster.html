Functional calculus for generators of semigroup operators in the class of Sato hyperfunctions

Perepichka N.

► In the dissertation study, it is planned to investigate the class of hyperfunctions with compact supports and Laplace hyperfunctions, as well as to construct a functional calculus in these classes for generators of strongly continuous and analytic semigroups of operators. In addition, admissible operations in the class of basic and generalized functions (hyperfunctions) will be investigated. Particular attention will be paid to the cross-correlation operation, on which the modification of the Laplace transform (the basis of functional calculus) is built. In each of the cases of functional calculus, it is planned to consider classes of practical problems that can be solved by the method of operator calculus.

► The results of the dissertation can be used in the theory of ordinary differential equations and equations with partial derivatives to solve ordinary and abstract Cauchy problems. In addition, applications can be found in quantum field theory.

 
 

► Hyperfunctions with compact supports and Laplace hyperfunctions, strongly continuous and analytic semigroups of operators, their generators and functions (hyperfunctions) from them.

► The work will use the methods of complex and functional analysis, in particular, the theory of analytical extension of functions of a complex variable, contour integration, the theory of duality of locally convex spaces, the theory of semigroups of operators in Banach spaces.

 
 

► The development of operator calculus began with the publication of the famous monograph by E. Hille "Functional analysis and semigroups". Since then, many works have been published on the topic of operator calculus for generators of strongly continuous and analytic semigroups of operators. E. Nelson and A. Balakrishnan extended the classical operator calculus to a wider class of analytic functions. P. Yara constructed an extension of Hille-Philips operator calculus for generators of bicontinuous semigroups. B. Boimer, M. Haase and M. Kovach showed the application of this type of calculation in hydrology, in particular to modeling the movement of near-surface (subsoil) waters.

► Schwartz's generalized functions revolutionized the field of theoretical and mathematical physics. The new theory made it possible to express in a mathematically correct form such idealized concepts as the density of a material point, the density of a point charge or dipole, the intensity of an instantaneous source, the intensity of the force applied to a point, etc. It is not surprising that new generalizations of the concept of function are currently being actively developed, among which Sato's hyperfunctions are particularly interesting.

► The theory of hyperfunctions is a useful tool in the study of D-modules, holonomic systems of differential equations and, especially, some aspects of symplectic geometry and harmonic analysis.

► Algebras of distributions with a symmetric tensor multiplication operation were used almost for the first time by H. Borchers in quantum field theory. This became the impetus for scientific research on problems related to all possible extensions of the cross-correlation of generalized functions (hyperfunctions) and the corresponding operator calculus.

► Solving the abstract Cauchy problem is a classical part of the theory of differential equations. The standard approach to solving such a problem for operators in Banach spaces is the use of the Laplace transform for operator-valued (generalized) functions. Among the various approaches, the idea of H. Komatsu, who proposed considering the Cauchy problem within the framework of the so-called Laplace hyperfunctions, is considered to be the most general.
